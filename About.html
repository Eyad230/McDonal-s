About McDonald's

McDonald's is one of the largest fast-food companies in the world. It was founded in 1940 by Richard McDonald and Maurice McDonald in California, USA.

In 1955, businessman Ray Kroc joined the company and helped it grow into a global brand. He later became the owner of McDonald's and expanded it to thousands of restaurants around the world.

Today, McDonald's serves millions of customers every day and is famous for its burgers, French fries, chicken meals, desserts, and drinks. The company has restaurants in more than 100 countries.
